Frequently Asked Inquiries

Clear, direct answers regarding corporate compliance timelines and statutory parameters in Nepal.

Under the Income Tax Act of Nepal, the standard corporate tax return must be filed within 3 months from the end of the income year (usually by Ashwin end). Extensions can be requested up to an additional 3 months upon valid legal justification approved by the Inland Revenue Department (IRD).

The Accounting Standards Board (ASB) of Nepal has systematically phased in NFRS for SMEs. While large commercial institutions and listed companies must comply with full NFRS, specified smaller entities can utilize modified NFRS for SMEs to reduce structural administrative burdens.
