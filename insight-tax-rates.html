Nepal tax rates at a glance: what applies to your business this fiscal year

By CA Priya Sharma · Tax Partner Published 2026-06-11 6 min read

Every budget season we field the same first question from clients: "did my rate change?" This reference collects the rates businesses ask us about most. It is a starting point for planning — the rate that actually applies to you depends on your entity type, industry concessions and the current Finance Act.

Verify before you file. Rates below reflect the long-standing structure of Nepal's Income Tax Act and VAT Act and are updated after each Finance Act. Concessions, rebates and thresholds change year to year — confirm the figure for your specific case with your tax advisor before acting on it.

Corporate income tax

Corporate income tax — general structure
Entity / activityRate
General businesses (normal rate)25%
Banks, financial institutions, insurance, telecom30%
Special industries (manufacturing), subject to conditions20%
Concessional rates — exports, hydropower, listed entities, underdeveloped-area operationsvaries

The concession landscape is where most planning value sits — and where most errors happen. Manufacturing, export income, hydropower and IT-service exports each carry their own rebate regimes with eligibility conditions that must be documented at filing time, not reconstructed during an assessment.

VAT

Value added tax
ItemRate / rule
Standard rate on taxable supplies13%
Exports of goods and services0% (zero-rated)
Schedule 1 items (basic goods, health, education…)exempt

The recurring pain point is not the rate but the cycle: return filing and payment deadlines, credit reconciliation against IRD records, and the zero-rating evidence file for exporters.

Withholding (TDS) — the ones that catch people out

Common withholding taxes
Payment typeTypical rate
Dividends (resident companies — final withholding)5%
Service fees to resident service providers15% / 1.5%*
Rent (to natural persons / entities)10%
Interest paid by banks to natural persons6%
Payments to non-residentsvaries by type & treaty

*The 1.5% rate applies to VAT-registered service providers against invoices — applying the wrong leg of this rule is the single most common TDS finding in our tax health-checks.

The deadlines that matter more than the rates

  • Advance tax instalments — Poush, Chaitra and Ashad: 40% / 70% / 100% of estimated annual liability.
  • Annual income tax return — within 3 months of fiscal year end (Ashoj), extendable by application.
  • VAT returns — monthly (or trimester for eligible taxpayers), by the 25th of the following Nepali month.
  • TDS deposit & e-TDS returns — within 25 days of the month of deduction.

Missing an instalment is more expensive than most rate inefficiencies: interest and fees compound quietly until the next assessment surfaces them.

What to do with this

If your effective tax rate is above the headline rate for your category, you are likely leaving documented deductions or concessions unused. A structured tax health-check — done before year-end, not after — is how the 22% effective-rate reduction in our manufacturing engagement was found.
